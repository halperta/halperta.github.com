Research Summary 5

Back to “Assignments”

Assignment Overview

Write a one-page (8.5x11) paper describing and analyzing the ethos and pathos of the article you wrote about in RS5.

Put your name in the top left hand corner and list the author’s name and the full citation information of the piece you’re summarizing; set margins at 1 inch, spacing at single, and font at 12 pt. Do not exceed a page in length. This should be about 500 words.

Specifics

This assignment asks you to consider two aspects of the text: ethos and pathos. It should have three sections.

In the introduction, briefly state your controversy and describe the position taken by the author.

In the second section, describe the “ethos” of the article by answering the following questions:

  • What kind of evidence does the author use in the article to demonstrate his or her authority to write about the controversy? (For example: The author is a scholar who studies this controversy. The author has a profession related to the controversy. The author lives in the location where the controversy is taking place. The author was personally effected by the controversy.)
  • What kind of sources does the author refer to in the article? (for example: scholarly research; newspapers; historical figures; family; colleagues)
  • To conclude this section, summarize how the author establishes her or his authority. What kind of audience is this author appealing to?

In the third section, identify one place where the author makes an emotional appeal. Describe the appeal: how is it made? What emotions does it call upon? What assumptions does it make about its audience? * For example: a short story about a child suffering from an illness evokes feelings of sadness and sympathy, and assumes that the audience was family-oriented. A claim about the “government takeover of america” provokes feelings of anxiety and fear, and assumes that the audience is politically inclined to small governments. A story about a teenager’s accomplishments provokes feelings of hope and pride, and assumes that the audience believes in the “American Dream.”
